<div id="s0001" class="NLM_sec NLM_sec-type_intro NLM_sec_level_1">
 <h2 id="_i2" class="section-heading-2">Introduction</h2>
 <p>Semantics extraction with unique degrees of the analyzed texts, consisting of phrases, sentences, and documents. Recently, researchers have concentrated on semantics which is the interaction between human language (Chowdhury <span class="ref-lnk lazy-ref"><a data-rid="cit0007" data-reflink="_i36 _i37" href="#"><span class="off-screen">Citation</span>2003</a></span>). Currently, a big collection of documents has been sent and saved electronically; however, there is a need to preprocess text to extract meaningful information. Semantics can define how different words have altered meanings for other people (Wolf <span class="ref-lnk lazy-ref"><a data-rid="cit0040" data-reflink="_i36 _i37" href="#"><span class="off-screen">Citation</span>1991</a></span>). Semantics analysis applications accomplished on social media such as professional networking services (LinkedIn), social networking sites (Facebook), media sharing networks (Instagram, Pinterest, YouTube), social blogging networks (Tumblr, Medium), discussion networks (Reddit, Quora,), and review networks (Yelp, Glassdoor). These enables people from all over the world to post and share images, videos, audios, and professional profile information through LinkedIn (Bontcheva and Rout <span class="ref-lnk lazy-ref"><a data-rid="cit0006" data-reflink="_i36 _i37" href="#"><span class="off-screen">Citation</span>2014</a></span>). Specifically, scholars performed sentiments analysis on several social media datasets, particularly healthcare datasets, Facebook comments, movies datasets, and tweets (Saif et al. <span class="ref-lnk lazy-ref"><a data-rid="cit0031" data-reflink="_i36 _i37" href="#"><span class="off-screen">Citation</span>2013</a></span>). Over the last decade, with the explosion of work for exploring various aspects of sentiment analysis: detecting subjective and objective sentences; classifying sentences as positive, negative, or neutral; detecting the person expressing the sentiment and the target of the sentiment analysis; detecting emotions such as joy, fear, and anger in the text. Surveys by (Liu and Zhang <span class="ref-lnk lazy-ref"><a data-rid="cit0023" data-reflink="_i36 _i37" href="#"><span class="off-screen">Citation</span>2012</a></span>) give a summary of various of these approaches. In today’s living world contexts, documents are stored electronically in every domain, as text data are increased highly in industry, business, technology, and the agriculture sector. Agriculture has become innovative by using IOT (Farooq et al. <span class="ref-lnk lazy-ref"><a data-rid="cit0012" data-reflink="_i36 _i37" href="#"><span class="off-screen">Citation</span>2020</a></span>), Cloud Computing (Mekala and Viswanathan <span class="ref-lnk lazy-ref"><a data-rid="cit0025" data-reflink="_i36 _i37" href="#"><span class="off-screen">Citation</span>2017</a></span>), Artificial Intelligence (Smith <span class="ref-lnk lazy-ref"><a data-rid="cit0034" data-reflink="_i36 _i37" href="#"><span class="off-screen">Citation</span>2018</a></span>), Machine Learning (Benos et al. <span class="ref-lnk lazy-ref"><a data-rid="cit0005" data-reflink="_i36 _i37" href="#"><span class="off-screen">Citation</span>2021</a></span>), Deep Learning, and Data Science (Angiani et al. <span class="ref-lnk lazy-ref"><a data-rid="cit0004" data-reflink="_i36 _i37" href="#"><span class="off-screen">Citation</span>2016</a></span>). Generally, agricultural productivity depends on some essential factors like fertilizer, seed, soil, water, and climate change (Ahmad and Heng <span class="ref-lnk lazy-ref"><a data-rid="cit0003" data-reflink="_i36 _i37" href="#"><span class="off-screen">Citation</span>2012</a></span>). In Pakistan, Punjab is the main agriculture zone of major and minor crops that contributes 18.9% of GDP and 42.3% of the labor force (Elahi et al. <span class="ref-lnk lazy-ref"><a data-rid="cit0011" data-reflink="_i36 _i37" href="#"><span class="off-screen">Citation</span>2020</a></span>). In the agriculture field only, we have had a large amount of text data through diverse platforms: Tweeter, Facebook, and LinkedIn groups (Martini et al. <span class="ref-lnk lazy-ref"><a data-rid="cit0024" data-reflink="_i36 _i37" href="#"><span class="off-screen">Citation</span>2011</a></span>). The scientists used semantics analysis on agriculture datasets to judge similarities, sentiments, emotions, feelings, and thoughts regarding crop productivity.</p>
 <p>Various techniques have been used for selecting and extracting features from text such as Frequency Features Term Frequency Inverse Document Frequency (TF-IDF), Count Vector, N-grams (Uni-Gram, Bi-Gram, Tri-Gram), and Bag of Words (BOW) (Mirończuk and Protasiewicz <span class="ref-lnk lazy-ref"><a data-rid="cit0026" data-reflink="_i36 _i37" href="#"><span class="off-screen">Citation</span>2018</a></span>). The Term Frequency and Inverse Document Frequency (TFIDF) have been widely used method for features extraction (Abualigah et al. <span class="ref-lnk lazy-ref"><a data-rid="cit0002" data-reflink="_i36 _i37" href="#"><span class="off-screen">Citation</span>2017</a></span>). Machine Learning has been currently implemented on soil types for agriculture crops productivity and management system (Saikai, Patel, and Mitchell <span class="ref-lnk lazy-ref"><a data-rid="cit0032" data-reflink="_i36 _i37" href="#"><span class="off-screen">Citation</span>2020</a></span>; Dongare et al. 2020). The deep learning approach is also covering many problems related to agriculture (Hoang et al. <span class="ref-lnk lazy-ref"><a data-rid="cit0015" data-reflink="_i36 _i37" href="#"><span class="off-screen">Citation</span>2013</a></span>), bioinformatics, and computational biology of plants, (Muharam et al. <span class="ref-lnk lazy-ref"><a data-rid="cit0027" data-reflink="_i36 _i37" href="#"><span class="off-screen">Citation</span>2021</a></span>). Many of the scholars have presented different ICT applications in agriculture in remote sensing, ecosystem service, crop yield forecasting, land monitoring climate change, and online demand of agricultural products (Abd-Elmabod et al. <span class="ref-lnk lazy-ref"><a data-rid="cit0001" data-reflink="_i36 _i37" href="#"><span class="off-screen">Citation</span>2020</a></span>; Weiss, Jacob, and Duveiller <span class="ref-lnk lazy-ref"><a data-rid="cit0039" data-reflink="_i36 _i37" href="#"><span class="off-screen">Citation</span>2020</a></span>; Kantasa-ard et al. <span class="ref-lnk lazy-ref"><a data-rid="cit0019" data-reflink="_i36 _i37" href="#"><span class="off-screen">Citation</span>2020</a></span>). Independently, scientists are applying machine learning techniques in agricultural input for measuring their effects (Benos et al. <span class="ref-lnk lazy-ref"><a data-rid="cit0005" data-reflink="_i36 _i37" href="#"><span class="off-screen">Citation</span>2021</a></span>).</p>
 <p>Semantics analysis has been used for the management of crop, soil, and water in the agriculture domain (Karthikeyan, et al., <span class="ref-lnk lazy-ref"><a data-rid="cit0020" data-reflink="_i36 _i37" href="#"><span class="off-screen">Citation</span>2020</a></span>). Many Agricultural applications like digital agriculture (Jayaraman et al. <span class="ref-lnk lazy-ref"><a data-rid="cit0018" data-reflink="_i36 _i37" href="#"><span class="off-screen">Citation</span>2015</a></span>) follow IOT infrastructure, which relates to the crop management system (Prathibha, Hongal, and Jyothi <span class="ref-lnk lazy-ref"><a data-rid="cit0029" data-reflink="_i36 _i37" href="#"><span class="off-screen">Citation</span>2017</a></span>). The focus of the proposed study was to apply a semantics analysis on agrarian opinion and providing their recommendations/guidelines for farmers that play a valuable role in crop growth and management. Therefore, an analysis of agricultural experts’ opinions toward crop productivity is present in this study. Major Contributions of the proposed work are:</p>
 <ul class="NLM_list NLM_list-list_type-bullet">
  <li><p class="inline">Collection of descriptive opinions of the agricultural experts through questionnaires</p></li>
  <li><p class="inline">Analyze the descriptive opinions of the agricultural experts through machine learning techniques.</p></li>
  <li><p class="inline">Determine the significant factors that affect agricultural productivity through opinion mining that are helpful to farmers and policymakers.</p></li>
 </ul>
 <p></p>
 <p>The rest of the paper is arranged as: <a href="#s0002">section 2</a> describes the proposed methodology and related materials. <a href="#s0003">Section 3</a> illustrates the experiment results and discussion. The conclusion is drawn in <a href="#s0004">section 4</a>.</p>

 <h2 id="_i3" class="section-heading-2">Materials and Methods</h2>
 <p>In this research text opinions, regarding agriculture productivity were collected from agrarians experts. After preprocessing, feature extraction techniques such as N-gram, BOW, TF, and TF-IDF were applied on corpus for informative features. KNN and Naïve Bayes algorithms were selected for training the model. In the end, a comparison between agrarians’ responses was carried out using the cosine similarity. <a href="#f0001">Figure 1</a> shows the flow of the system for the proposed study.</p>

  <h3 class="section-heading-3" id="_i5">Data Collection</h3>
  <p>A questionnaire consisting of 10 questions was designed with the help of agricultural and social science experts regarding agriculture productivity: soil, seed, pest, insects, water shortage, climate change, precision agriculture, synthetic fertilizer, post-harvest, and government policies. Google Form and Word Document were created and shared with respondents using social media. Respondents were agrarian experts from different DAI/institutes, Academia, Industry, and Research institutes. The experts were specialists in Agronomy, Soil, and Environmental Sciences, Entomology, Plant Breeding and Genetics, Biotechnology, Seed Science and Technology, Forestry, Climate Change and Horticulture, Plant Pathology, Cotton, Crop Nutrients Tillage, Development of Transgenic Crops, Water Treatment, and Oxidation, Plant Growth Promoting Rhizobacteria, Plant Physiology, Tissue Culture, and Biochemistry. Proformas were disseminated at UAF (University of Agriculture Faisalabad), Bahauddin Zakariya University Multan (BZU), University of Gujrat (UAG), MNS University of Agriculture, Multan (MNSUAM), Cotton Center Research Institute Multan (CCRI), and National Institute for Biotechnology Genetic Engineering (NIBGE). Approximately one hundred responses were collected from agricultural experts, and the same is being continued for better results from the trained model. <button class="ref showTableEventRef" data-id="t0001">Table 1</button> shows the list of questions that were prepared for data collection from agricultural experts.</p>

  <h3 class="section-heading-3" id="_i6">Preprocessing</h3>
  <p>Preprocessing is the process of scrubbing and preparing the text for classification. The text consists of implicit noise that needs to be removed using data cleaning techniques. In the present research, preprocessing techniques such as tokenization of words, stop words removal, stemming, lemmatization, and a bag of words (Singh and Kumari <span class="ref-lnk lazy-ref"><a data-rid="cit0033" data-reflink="_i36 _i37" href="#"><span class="off-screen">Citation</span>2016</a></span>) were applied. Tokenization was used to convert text into chunks. It is necessary to remove such words from the corpus; therefore, stop words have been used that are ‘a,’ ‘an, ‘the,’ ‘have,’ ‘has,’ ‘from,’ ‘we,’ ‘will,’ ‘they,’ ‘them,’ and much more. Similar stemming, also called lemmatization, has been used. Lemmatization removed the suffix of a word entirely and obtained the basic word form (lemma) (Kowsari et al. <span class="ref-lnk lazy-ref"><a data-rid="cit0021" data-reflink="_i36 _i37" href="#"><span class="off-screen">Citation</span>2019</a></span>). Count vector defined by several occurrences of features a basic way to represent the text data numerically called one-hot encoding (count vectorization) (Vaghela, Jadav, and Scholar <span class="ref-lnk lazy-ref"><a data-rid="cit0037" data-reflink="_i36 _i37" href="#"><span class="off-screen">Citation</span>2016</a></span>). Word cloud is also called text cloud/tags clouds, generated from the source of textual data in which words are depicted in different sizes. Word clouds are an alternative way of analyzing text from online surveys and documents, which is much faster than coding Essentially, word clouds generators work by breaking the text down into component words and counting how frequently they appear in the context-based documents, as shown in <a href="#f0002">Figure 2</a>.</p>

 <div id="s0002-s2003" class="NLM_sec NLM_sec_level_2">
  <h3 class="section-heading-3" id="_i8">Features Extraction</h3>
  <p>Text documents aim to select features from text to determine the most informative features that contain high dimensional informative (Abualigah et al. <span class="ref-lnk lazy-ref"><a data-rid="cit0002" data-reflink="_i36 _i37" href="#"><span class="off-screen">Citation</span>2017</a></span>). Researchers used divergent approaches to extract features from the corpus (Mirończuk and Protasiewicz <span class="ref-lnk lazy-ref"><a data-rid="cit0026" data-reflink="_i36 _i37" href="#"><span class="off-screen">Citation</span>2018</a></span>), Continuous Words (CBOW) with Skip-Grams, Term Frequency or Inverse Document Frequency (TF-IDF), Features Frequency (FF), N-grams, Word frequency and weight calculation intensity words, negation words, and overall sentence weight calculation to find poultry of words with negative or positive weights (Razzaq et al. <span class="ref-lnk lazy-ref"><a data-rid="cit0030" data-reflink="_i36 _i37" href="#"><span class="off-screen">Citation</span>2019</a></span>). Many methods exist that can be chosen according to the dataset requirements (Mirończuk and Protasiewicz <span class="ref-lnk lazy-ref"><a data-rid="cit0026" data-reflink="_i36 _i37" href="#"><span class="off-screen">Citation</span>2018</a></span>). In this current study, we have selected Bag of words (BOW), Term Frequency, N-Gram, TF-IDF for feature selection. BOW applies on the text document, and N-gram (Uni, Bi, and Tri-Grams) approach was used to find the impact of those words that repeat mostly with higher frequency from “Agrarian Experts.”</p>

  <h3 class="section-heading-3" id="_i23">Model Training and Evaluation</h3>
  <p>In literature, various machine learning algorithms have been used for semantics analysis such as K-Nearest Neighbors (Hmeidi, Hawashin, and El-Qawasmeh <span class="ref-lnk lazy-ref"><a data-rid="cit0014" data-reflink="_i36 _i37" href="#"><span class="off-screen">Citation</span>2008</a></span>), Support Vector Machine (Cortes and Vapnik <span class="ref-lnk lazy-ref"><a data-rid="cit0008" data-reflink="_i36 _i37" href="#"><span class="off-screen">Citation</span>1995</a></span>), Neural Networks, Decision Tree, and Naive Bayes (Xia and Wang <span class="ref-lnk lazy-ref"><a data-rid="cit0041" data-reflink="_i36 _i37" href="#"><span class="off-screen">Citation</span>2004</a></span>). We applied K-Nearest Neighbors and Naïve Bayes algorithms for the classification of the text. Both algorithms performed better and obtained reasonable accuracy of the K-Nearst Neighbors and Naïve Bayes 84% and 87%, respectively tabulated in <button class="ref showTableEventRef" data-id="t0002">Table 2</button>. The machine was trained using agrarians’ opinions and to test the classifier predicated results. Furthermore, finally, the model has been tested on different agriculture inputs: Soil, Seed Type, Pest, Plant, Diseases, Irrigation, Climate, Precision, Fertilizer, Harvest, and Policy and received relevant results of the queries. Moreover, model has evaluated by considering the accuracy, precision, recall, F1 Score (Wang and Li <span class="ref-lnk lazy-ref"><a data-rid="cit0038" data-reflink="_i36 _i37" href="#"><span class="off-screen">Citation</span>2019</a></span>).</p>

  <h3 class="section-heading-3" id="_i32">Discussion</h3>
  <p>Over the last decade, scientists were focused on crop inputs for finding their role in crop productivity such as soil, soil types, soil humidity (Dongare <span class="ref-lnk lazy-ref"><a data-rid="cit0010" data-reflink="_i36 _i37" href="#"><span class="off-screen">Citation</span>2020</a></span>), fertilizer management ((Saikai, Patel, and Mitchell <span class="ref-lnk lazy-ref"><a data-rid="cit0032" data-reflink="_i36 _i37" href="#"><span class="off-screen">Citation</span>2020</a></span>), crop management, seed, water temperature, climate changes, sustainability, chemical spry (Karthikeyan et al. Karthikeyan, et al., <span class="ref-lnk lazy-ref"><a data-rid="cit0020" data-reflink="_i36 _i37" href="#"><span class="off-screen">Citation</span>2020</a></span>). The present study has been conducted on “Agriculture Semantics Analysis” through machine learning model. The proposed study may also help in developing agricultural policies at the government level and a comparison is also made to find similarities between agrarians experts opinions. This research demonstrated that Naïve Bayes had better performed on experts opinions textual data. Supervised techniques like support vector machine, neural network, decision tree, random forest was not applied due to small dataset.The limitations of the proposed study are that the sample size needs to add more responses. There is a lack of previous research to compare and develop a real-time platform for disseminating findings to farmer communities.</p>

<div id="s0004" class="NLM_sec NLM_sec_level_1">
 <h2 id="_i33" class="section-heading-2">Conclusion</h2>
 <p>In this research, we have presented a novel approach for collecting and analyzing the descriptive opinions of agricultural experts. The study has shown that <b>Crop, Certified Seed, and Post-Harvest Loss</b> are the significantly contribute to agricultural productivity. Similarities between agrarian’s responses that belonged to different Institute/DAI, Academia have determined through cosine similarity and document comparison method.. This study was carried out using machine learning algorithm such as Naïve Byes and KNN algorithm and obtained 87% and 84% accuracy respectively. This study demonstrated that Naïve Byes has better performed better on text dataset of agriculture experts opinions.</p>
